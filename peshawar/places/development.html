Warsak Dam

Warsak Dam (Urdu: ورسک ڈیم) is a mass concrete gravity dam located on the Kabul River in District Mohmand, Mechni, approximately 30 km northwest from the city of Peshawar in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan.

Overview of History

Warsak Dam was completed under the Colombo Plan in two phases and financed by the Canadian Government.The first phase was completed in 1960 and consisted of the construction of the dam. Irrigation tunnels and installation of four power generating units, each of 40 MW capacity with 132 kV transmission system, were also completed in 1960. Two additional generating units of 41.48 MW capacity each were added in 1980-81 in the second phase.

Peshawar Zoo

Peshawar Zoo (Urdu: پشاور چڑیا گھر) is the first zoo established in Peshawar and one of the largest in Pakistan. It officially opened on 12th February 2018 and is managed by the Forests, Environment, and Wildlife Department of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa.

History

The zoo’s construction began on 3rd February 2016, with a groundbreaking by Imran Khan and CM Pervez Khattak. Spread over 29 acres beside the Pakistan Forest Institute and Markaz-Ul-Uloom Islamia Rahatabad, it opened in February 2018.

Initial animal arrivals included lions from Lahore Zoo, leopards from Dhodial Pheasantry, and a bear from Nathiagali. In 2018, Bengal tigers were imported from South Africa, followed by giraffes, a snake house, and Arabian oryx from the UAE. Early challenges included animal care issues due to harsh weather and limited medical facilities.

Main Attractions

  • Lion House: Indoor and moated outdoor enclosures for lions and bears.
  • Bird Aviary: Walk-through aviary (partly under development) featuring pelicans, flamingos, pheasants, bustards, and cranes.
  • Leopard House: Enclosures for leopards, macaques, wolves, and Bengal tigers.

Animal Collection

Mammals

Includes lions, Bengal tigers, leopards, wolves, zebras, giraffes, llamas, camels, deer species, Arabian oryx, Blackbuck, Markhor, and more.

Birds

A wide range including ostriches, flamingos, pelicans, cranes, pheasants, falcons, parrots, and macaws.

Reptiles

Features pythons, cobras, corn snakes, crocodiles, turtles, and vipers.

Location

Located on University Road, near Arbab Niaz Cricket Stadium and Shahi Bagh, the zoo is easily accessible from all parts of the city. Nearby landmarks include Peshawar Museum, Islamia College, and University of Peshawar.
